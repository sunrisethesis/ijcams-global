Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis
Biochemical and nutritional parameters as predictors of weaning failure in mechanically ventilated patients: a systematic review and meta-analysis
Sharma N, Verma AK, Singh P, et al.  ·  PROSPERO Registered (CRD Pending)
Background: Weaning failure from mechanical ventilation remains a major challenge in intensive care. Nutritional and biochemical parameters may serve as objective predictors. Methods: A systematic search of PubMed, Scopus, and Cochrane was performed. Studies reporting biochemical or nutritional markers in relation to weaning outcomes were included. Results: Pooled analysis showed significant association of serum albumin, prealbumin, phosphate, magnesium, and NRS-2002 scores with weaning failure. Conclusions: Biochemical and nutritional screening should be integrated into pre-weaning assessment protocols.

01
Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis
Biochemical and nutritional parameters as predictors of weaning failure in mechanically ventilated patients: a systematic review and meta-analysis
Sharma N, Verma AK, Singh P, et al.  ·  MMIMSR Mullana, Haryana  ·  PROSPERO Registered
Background: Weaning failure from mechanical ventilation occurs in 20–30% of ICU patients and significantly increases morbidity, mortality, and cost. Nutritional deficiencies and biochemical derangements are increasingly recognised as modifiable predictors. This systematic review and meta-analysis synthesises evidence on the role of serum albumin, prealbumin, electrolytes (phosphate, magnesium, potassium), inflammatory markers, and nutritional risk scores (NRS-2002, NUTRIC score) as objective predictors of weaning outcomes. Methods: PubMed, Scopus, and Cochrane CENTRAL were searched from inception to December 2025. Results: 24 studies met inclusion criteria (n=6,847 patients). Hypoalbuminaemia (OR 2.84, 95%CI 1.94–4.16) and hypophosphataemia (OR 3.12, 95%CI 2.01–4.85) were the strongest predictors of weaning failure. Conclusions: Routine biochemical and nutritional assessment should be incorporated into pre-weaning protocols in ICU.

02
Original Research
Comparative analysis of homatropine 2% versus tropicamide 1% on anterior segment parameters: a prospective randomised study
Gupta R, Singh P, Agarwal M, et al.  ·  Sharda University, Greater Noida
Purpose: To compare the effect of homatropine 2% and tropicamide 1% on pupil diameter, anterior chamber depth, anterior chamber angle, and intraocular pressure using anterior segment OCT and Goldmann applanation tonometry. Methods: 100 eyes of 50 patients were randomised to receive homatropine 2% or tropicamide 1%. Parameters were recorded at baseline, 30 min, and 60 min post-instillation. Results: Tropicamide produced faster onset of mydriasis (mean 22.3 min vs 34.6 min, p<0.001). Homatropine produced greater cycloplegia at 60 minutes. Anterior chamber angle narrowing was clinically significant only in pre-existing shallow angles. IOP did not change significantly in either group in phakic eyes. Conclusions: Clinical selection of mydriatic agent should be based on purpose of use and individual anatomical risk factors.

Manuscript Formatting

General

  • Language: English (British or American, consistently used)
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt, double-spaced throughout
  • Margins: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
  • Page numbers: Bottom right on all pages
  • Line numbers: Continuous line numbers must be added
  • File format: Microsoft Word (.doc or .docx); PDF for figures

Structure of Original Research Papers

  1. Title Page — Title, authors with degrees & affiliations, corresponding author, conflict of interest, funding, ethics approval number, CTRI/trial registration
  2. Abstract — Structured: Background, Aims/Objectives, Methods, Results, Conclusions, Keywords (5–8, MeSH terms preferred)
  3. Introduction — Background, rationale, hypothesis, objectives
  4. Materials & Methods — Study design, setting, period, participants (inclusion/exclusion), sample size calculation, intervention, outcome measures, statistical methods, ethical approval
  5. Results — Present data with tables and figures; avoid duplicating data in text
  6. Discussion — Interpret findings, compare with literature, limitations, future directions
  7. Conclusions — Brief, based only on the data presented
  8. Acknowledgements — List funding agencies, technical support, statisticians
  9. Conflict of Interest Statement
  10. References
  11. Tables — Each on a separate page with title above and footnotes below
  12. Figures/Images — High resolution (≥300 DPI for photographs, ≥600 DPI for line art); submitted separately as TIFF/JPEG

Reference Style

IJCAMS follows the Vancouver referencing style (ICMJE format). References are numbered consecutively in the order they appear in the text, cited in superscript numerals.

Journal Article: Smith A, Jones B, Patel R. Title of the article. Journal Name Abbrev. Year;Volume(Issue):Page–Page.

Book: Last FM, editor. Title of book. Edition. Place: Publisher; Year.

Online Source: Author AA. Title of article [Internet]. Year [cited Year Month Day]. Available from: URL

Authors are responsible for verifying all references against PubMed or the original source before submission. Unverified or fabricated references will lead to immediate rejection.

Tables & Figures

  • Tables must be created in MS Word (not pasted as images)
  • Table titles appear above the table; footnotes below (using superscript letters: a, b, c)
  • Figures: high-resolution JPEG/TIFF (≥300 DPI); each submitted as a separate file
  • Figure legends are listed on a separate page at the end of the manuscript
  • All abbreviations in tables/figures must be defined in footnotes
  • Photomicrographs must include scale bars and stain name
  • Patient-identifiable information must be removed from clinical images; written consent required

Pre-Submission Checklist

  • ☐ Title page is separate from the main manuscript (blinded review)
  • ☐ Structured abstract with keywords included
  • ☐ Line numbers added (continuous)
  • ☐ Double-spacing, Times New Roman 12pt
  • ☐ Vancouver references verified against PubMed
  • ☐ Ethics approval number stated in Methods section
  • ☐ CTRI/trial registration number stated (for clinical trials)
  • ☐ PRISMA checklist attached (for systematic reviews/meta-analyses)
  • ☐ PROSPERO registration mentioned (for SR/MA)
  • ☐ CONSORT checklist attached (for RCTs)
  • ☐ CARE checklist attached (for case reports)
  • ☐ Conflict of interest statement completed
  • ☐ Author contribution statement included
  • ☐ Patient consent obtained and documented (case reports)
  • ☐ Figures submitted at ≥300 DPI as separate JPEG/TIFF files
  • ☐ Plagiarism below 15% (self-checked via iThenticate/Turnitin)
  • ☐ Cover letter uploaded

Peer Review Process

IJCAMS uses a double-blind peer review system. Manuscripts are first screened by the Editor-in-Chief for scope and quality. Manuscripts passing initial screening are sent to two or more independent reviewers.

Possible Decisions

  • Accept as is — Rare for first submission; paper is accepted without changes
  • Minor revision — Small changes required; authors given 2 weeks
  • Major revision — Substantial changes required; authors given 4 weeks; re-review possible
  • Reject with resubmission invited — Fundamental issues but concept is sound
  • Reject outright — Does not meet scope or quality threshold

Ethical Requirements

  • IEC/IRB approval number must be stated for all clinical studies
  • Clinical trials must be registered in CTRI (India) or ClinicalTrials.gov
  • Written informed consent required for all case reports and studies involving identifiable patients
  • All animal experiments must follow CPCSEA / IAEC guidelines
  • Authors must follow ICMJE authorship criteria strictly

About IJCAMS

Learn about our mission, scope, ethics policies, and commitment to open-access medical publishing.

Our Mission

International Journal of Clinical and Applied Medical Sciences (IJCAMS) is committed to the rapid, ethical, and free dissemination of high-quality medical research. We serve as a convergence point where clinical evidence meets applied practice across every discipline of medicine. We believe that scientific knowledge should be freely accessible to all, which is why we charge no article processing fee and impose no subscription barriers.

Aims & Scope

IJCAMS is a peer-reviewed, open-access, multidisciplinary medical journal published quarterly by Sunrise Research Works, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India. The journal publishes original research, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, case reports, narrative reviews, clinical images, editorials, letters to the editor, and short communications across all specialties of clinical and applied medicine.

The journal welcomes submissions from researchers, clinicians, and allied health professionals from India and across the world. We particularly encourage submissions from postgraduate medical residents, junior faculty, and clinician-scientists who are building their research careers.

Subject Coverage

The journal covers — but is not limited to — the following areas:

  • Internal Medicine, Endocrinology, Rheumatology
  • Respiratory Medicine, Critical Care, and Pulmonology
  • Surgery (General, Orthopaedic, Cardiothoracic, Neurosurgery)
  • Obstetrics & Gynaecology, Reproductive Medicine
  • Paediatrics & Neonatology, Paediatric Neurology
  • Ophthalmology & Visual Sciences
  • ENT, Head & Neck Surgery, Audiology
  • Dermatology & Venereology
  • Radiology, Imaging & Interventional Procedures
  • Pathology, Histopathology, Cytology & Haematology
  • Anatomy & Morphometry (Clinical Anatomy)
  • Pharmacology & Clinical Therapeutics
  • Psychiatry & Mental Health
  • Community Medicine, Epidemiology & Biostatistics
  • Dentistry & Oral Medicine
  • Nursing & Allied Health Sciences

Publication Ethics & COPE Compliance

IJCAMS adheres strictly to the guidelines of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE). All submissions are expected to meet the ethical standards described below. Violations will result in rejection or retraction.

Authorship
All listed authors must meet ICMJE criteria for authorship: substantial contribution to conception, design, data acquisition, analysis, and interpretation; drafting or critically revising the manuscript; approving the final version; and accountability for all aspects of the work.
Plagiarism
All manuscripts are screened for plagiarism using iThenticate / Turnitin. A similarity index of >15% (excluding references) will require revision. Manuscripts with >25% similarity may be rejected. Self-plagiarism is also not permitted.
Ethics Approval
Studies involving human participants must have Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC/IRB) approval. Clinical trials must be registered in a recognised trial registry (CTRI, ClinicalTrials.gov). Informed consent must be documented for case reports.
Conflict of Interest
All authors must disclose any financial or non-financial conflicts of interest. Funding sources, pharmaceutical company involvement, and any competing interests must be declared on the title page and in the manuscript.
Data Fabrication
Fabrication or falsification of data, including manipulation of images or statistical results, is a serious breach of ethics. The journal reserves the right to retract any published article where such misconduct is established post-publication.
Duplicate Submission
Simultaneous submission of the same manuscript to more than one journal is not permitted. Manuscripts previously published (in whole or in part) in any language must be disclosed. Preprint server deposits (e.g., medRxiv) must be declared at submission.

Open Access Policy

IJCAMS is a fully open-access journal. All articles are freely available online immediately upon publication without any subscription or paywall. Authors retain copyright under a Creative Commons Attribution Non-Commercial 4.0 International (CC BY-NC 4.0) license, which allows free reading, sharing, and adaptation for non-commercial purposes with appropriate attribution.

IJCAMS charges no Article Processing Charge (APC). There is no submission fee, no review fee, and no publication fee. Publishing in IJCAMS is completely free for all authors.

Peer Review Process

IJCAMS follows a rigorous double-blind peer review process. Author identities are concealed from reviewers and reviewer identities from authors. Each manuscript is reviewed by a minimum of two independent experts in the relevant specialty. The editorial board reserves the right to reject manuscripts without external review if they do not meet the basic standards of the journal or fall outside the scope.

Review Timeline

  • Initial editorial screening: 5–7 working days
  • External peer review: 4–6 weeks
  • Revision turnaround expected from authors: 4 weeks
  • Final editorial decision: 2–4 weeks after revision
  • Production and online publication: 2–3 weeks after acceptance
